Section 2.1: Defining Behavioural Strategy

Behavioural strategy merges strategic management theory with insights from cognitive and social psychology. It acknowledges that strategic choices are never purely objective — they are the final output of invisible mental filters.

The Dual-Process Framework — System 1 vs System 2

System 1 — Fast Thinking

  • Automatic, intuitive, emotionally driven
  • Operates below conscious awareness
  • Draws on pattern recognition
  • Vulnerable to cognitive biases
  • Dominant in high-pressure sessions

System 2 — Slow Thinking

  • Deliberate, analytical, effortful
  • Requires conscious attention
  • Analyses data, weighs trade-offs
  • Easily overridden under time pressure
  • Requires psychological safety to activate

Section 2.3: The 4 Bias Families (20 Types)

Action-Oriented · Stability · Interest · Social — each representing a distinct way judgement becomes systematically skewed. Facilitator note: do not lecture through all 20. Use the table as a reference. Focus on 3–4 that resonate with the group's organisational context.

Synthesis Prompt — Converging Zone

Elephant Perspective Exercise

Assign each participant a CXO role. Ask them to read the same strategic scenario and write their "internal translation." Surface in plenary. Use to demonstrate functional bias before naming it.

  1. Assign CXO roles (CEO, CFO, COO, CHRO, CTO, CMO, CCO, CPO, CRO, CSO)
  2. Distribute the MyHealth Live Portal scenario
  3. Each CXO writes their "internal translation" — what success looks like from their seat
  4. Plenary share — facilitator maps responses on whiteboard/screen
  5. Debrief: where do the translations align? Where do they fragment?

Chapter 3: Why Behavioural Strategy Is Critical

The hidden cost of poor behavioural awareness manifests as misallocated capital, strategic inertia, execution failure, innovation suppression, and purpose drift. Each maps directly to a bias family.

The White Space Concept

The gap between functional mandates where no one is technically "in charge" but where the customer experience actually lives. Facilitator: use the MyHealth Portal example — every CXO hit their target, yet patient satisfaction collapsed.

Facilitator Action — Panoramic Scan

Three-Point White Space Check

  1. Does my "Done" actually create more work for you? (Vertical Success)
  2. If the system is Green but the customer is Red, where do we neutralise? (White Space)
  3. Has the enterprise won? (Enterprise Consequence)

Chapter 4: The Strategy Journey as a Bias Landscape

Four phases — Diagnosis, Option Generation, Decision, Execution — each activates a different cluster of biases. The four Scanner Questions anchor each phase for participant reflection.

Phase 1 · Diagnosis

  • Availability Bias
  • Anchoring Bias
  • Confirmation Bias
  • Scanner: Strip what we already believe — what does the evidence actually say?

Phase 2 · Option Generation

  • Status Quo Bias
  • Silo Bias
  • Career-Risk Bias
  • Scanner: What is the most disruptive option not yet on the table?

Phase 3 · Decision

  • Overconfidence Bias
  • Loss Aversion
  • Authority Bias / Halo Effect
  • Scanner: Would we make the same call if the proposer had no stake in the outcome?

Phase 4 · Execution

  • Planning Fallacy
  • Incentive Bias
  • Self-Interest Bias
  • Scanner: Are we measuring what matters, or what is easy to measure?

Facilitator Script — Converging Zone

"The quality of your strategy is a direct function of the quality of the thinking environment in which it was made. The Converging Zone is where we shift from individual expertise to collective intelligence."

Chapter 6: The Three Pillars of Convergence

1 · Cognitive Integration

  • Break functional silos
  • Build a single version of the truth
  • Stress test: if Finance data is right, does Marketing plan still hold?

2 · Behavioural Guardrails

  • Build friction into the meeting
  • Challenge Clock: 10 mins finding reasons the plan won't work
  • Stop the loudest voice winning by default

3 · Emotional Stabilisation

  • Name the feeling before the content
  • Move team from defensiveness to curiosity
  • Leaders must admit they don't have all answers

Institutional Practices

  • 72-Hour Pre-Read Mandate
  • Rotating Neutral Facilitation
  • Blind Ideation
  • Silent Deliberation before verbal discussion

Section 7.1: The Growth Decision — Converging Zone Simulation

Company: Apex Manufacturing. The board has placed pressure to accelerate growth while maintaining financial stability and operational integrity.

Phase 1 — Fragmented Reality (Distribute separately to groups)

Group 1 · Finance

  • Margins declining 2 years
  • Capital constraints tightening
  • Debt capacity limited
  • Reality: Financial sustainability under pressure

Group 2 · Market & Strategy

  • Adjacent markets growing faster
  • Competitors expanding aggressively
  • Strong acquisition targets available
  • Reality: Growth acceleration critical

Group 3 · Operations

  • Plants near full capacity
  • Integration capability limited
  • Previous integration effort struggled
  • Reality: Operational system stretched and fragile

Group 4 · People & Culture

  • Leadership bandwidth constrained
  • Change fatigue visible
  • Talent retention becoming concern
  • Reality: Organisation may not absorb further strain

Facilitator Debrief Questions

After Phase 2 Board Options are revealed

  1. When did you form your preferred option?
  2. What shaped that preference?
  3. What changed after seeing all perspectives?
  4. What made integration difficult?
