  • Distinguish the three observable friction signals: Decision Paralysis, Kinetic Misalignment, and the Output-Outcome Gap
  • Map the 7 clog categories to the ABCV element each distorts and the strategic impact each produces
  • Apply the system logic principle: clogs redirect effort, weaken coherence, and create misinterpretation of strategic intent
What is a Strategic Clog?
Core concept

At its simplest, a Strategic Clog is an internal blockage that prevents strategic intent from flowing into real-world execution. It is the widening gap between what we said we would do and what is actually happening.

Strategy sets the destination. Flow determines whether you have the power to arrive.

To navigate this phase of the execution climb, leadership must evolve. The role of the leadership team becomes to serve as Engineers of Execution Flow — developing the diagnostic eyes to detect clogs early, before they compound into stalled momentum or declining performance.

Every clog interferes with one or more elements of ABCV Directional Integrity:

  • Arena — clarity of the problem space and customer end-game
  • Boundaries — structural, operational, and regulatory constraints
  • Competition — how the organisation defines alternatives and threats
  • Value Proposition — how the organisation creates distinct value

When execution is clogged, strategy does not fail visibly. It loses directional integrity — gradually and silently. A clog acts like a pressure distortion point: it redirects effort away from intended outcomes, weakens coherence across functions, and creates misinterpretation of strategic intent.

The 7-Clog Diagnostic Map
ABCV-aligned · Select each clog

Each clog category represents a distinct type of system misalignment. Select each to explore, and reflect on which clogs are currently present in your organisation.

Clog 01
Structural Misfit
Boundaries
▼
What It Looks Like
Org design, roles, reporting lines, decision rights, or governance layers are misaligned to strategy. Structure reflects legacy priorities, not strategic intent.
Early Warnings: Increasing coordination time; dependency on informal workarounds; escalation frequency rises; unclear ownership of outcomes.
Strategic Impact: Execution becomes disjointed; coordination costs increase; strategy cannot move coherently across the enterprise.
Reflect: Where in your structure do handoffs regularly break down? What does that tell you about the misalignment between your design and your strategy?
Clog 02
Capability Gaps
Arena
▼
What It Looks Like
Critical capabilities required to deliver the strategy are absent, underdeveloped, or unevenly distributed across the organisation.
Early Warnings: Missed milestones; uneven performance across similar initiatives; burnout of key talent; increasing rework.
Strategic Impact: The defined arena becomes theoretical — execution lacks the capability backbone required to compete and deliver.
Reflect: Which capability does your current strategy demand that your organisation has not yet fully built? How long has that gap been visible?
Clog 03
Decision Bottlenecks
Boundaries
▼
What It Looks Like
Decision rights are unclear, overly centralised, or embedded in outdated hierarchical processes. Authority is not aligned to execution proximity.
Early Warnings: Decision cycle times increase; growing backlog of unresolved issues; teams hesitate to act without approval.
Strategic Impact: Decision flow is constrained, weakening execution rhythm. The organisation loses responsiveness.
Reflect: Where in your organisation are decisions taking longer than the pace of strategy demands? What is the cost of that delay?
Clog 04
Misaligned Metrics & Incentives
Value Proposition
▼
What It Looks Like
KPIs, scorecards, and reward systems reinforce behaviours that conflict with strategic intent. Legacy metrics remain active post-strategy shift.
Early Warnings: High KPI achievement with weak strategic progress; conflicting performance narratives; misalignment between reported success and actual outcomes.
Strategic Impact: Effort redirected toward what is measured rather than what creates value. The organisation produces activity, not differentiated strategic outcomes.
Reflect: Which metrics in your current scorecard are rewarding behaviours that actually conflict with your strategic priorities?
Clog 05
Cultural Resistance
Value Proposition
▼
What It Looks Like
Deeply embedded beliefs, norms, and habits contradict the behavioural shifts required by the strategy. Informal culture overrides formal direction.
Early Warnings: Low engagement in strategic initiatives; limited experimentation; silence in critical conversations; informal narratives contradicting strategy.
Strategic Impact: The organisation cannot consistently deliver intended value because behaviour does not align with strategic intent.
Reflect: Is what you are observing genuinely a culture issue — or is it a Leadership Avoidance issue that has produced cultural symptoms?
Clog 06
Technology / Infrastructure Lag
Competition
▼
What It Looks Like
Legacy systems, fragmented platforms, and poorly integrated infrastructure cannot support execution demands. Data architecture is incomplete or delayed.
Early Warnings: Data inconsistencies; long reporting cycles; operational errors increase at scale; inability to track real-time performance.
Strategic Impact: The organisation loses execution speed and intelligence; cannot respond, scale, or compete against more technologically enabled alternatives.
Reflect: Which technology or infrastructure constraint is most directly limiting your ability to execute the strategy at the speed it demands?
Clog 07
Leadership Avoidance
Arena + Boundaries
▼
What It Looks Like
Leaders avoid confronting trade-offs, underperformance, structural misalignment, or difficult decisions required for strategy execution.
Early Warnings: Recurring strategic issues across multiple cycles; low trust in leadership decisions; ambiguity in direction; declining ownership across teams.
Strategic Impact: Strategic clarity erodes as unresolved tensions accumulate. The organisation drifts between priorities, weakening both focus and execution discipline.
Reflect: What difficult decision has your leadership team been circling for more than two cycles without closure? What is the real cost of continued avoidance?

Intelligence · Why

Why Clogs Are Predictable Features, Not Random Failures

Understanding why execution friction is the inevitable consequence of system misalignment — and the 7 levers that restore execution flow.

◆ Learning Objectives
  • Explain why clogs are not accidents but predictable consequences of strategy stretching the operating system
  • Shift from reactive troubleshooting to proactive execution architecture as your primary leadership mindset
  • Apply the three disciplines: early identification, accurate classification, targeted intervention
  • Match the 7 Unclogging Levers to their strategic roles and target clogs
Clogs Are Design Signals, Not Operational Failures

Execution breakdowns rarely occur because the strategy itself is incorrect. They occur because the organisation’s structure, capabilities, behaviours, or systems have not yet adapted to the strategy. The clog is not the failure. The clog is the signal that the system requires adjustment.

Clogs emerge precisely where strategy stretches the existing operating system beyond its current design capacity. They are predictable — not random. This transforms the role of the executive team: not only defining direction, but ensuring the operating system can carry that direction without excessive friction.

The mindset shift: instead of asking “Why is the strategy failing?” ask “Where is the system not yet designed to carry the strategy?”

The 7 Strategic Unclogging Levers
Select each lever

These seven levers are the leadership toolkit for execution architecture. They restore the conditions that allow the strategy to move through the organisation at the speed and quality intended.

Lever 01
Standardise
Ensure Consistency
▼

Harmonises processes and service levels across teams so everyone plays by the same rules. Reduces duplication and ensures every unit pulls in the same direction.

Apply when: Variability across teams is creating drag. Best for: Structural Misfit, Misaligned Metrics, Tech Lag.
Lever 02
Streamline
Increase Speed
▼

Simplifies workflows and removes bureaucratic layers that add no value. Leaner processes mean faster approvals and greater strategic agility.

Apply when: Approval processes are creating latency. Best for: Decision Bottlenecks, Structural Misfit.
Lever 03
Clarify
Eliminate Confusion
▼

Defines who owns what, where the handoffs are, and who has the final say. When people know their roles and decision rights, accountability returns.

Apply when: Ownership ambiguity is causing hesitation. Best for: Decision Bottlenecks, Structural Misfit, Leadership Avoidance.
Lever 04
Upgrade
Remove Bottlenecks
▼

Fixes underperforming technology and infrastructure that holds execution back. You cannot run a modern strategy on yesterday’s tools.

Apply when: Systems are creating friction rather than enabling flow. Best for: Technology / Infrastructure Lag, Capability Gaps.
Lever 05
Reinforce
Strengthen Behaviour
▼

Equips the team with the skills, coaching, and confidence needed to deliver. Strategy requires new behaviours — reinforcement ensures people are capable, not just willing.

Apply when: Skills or confidence are the limiting factor. Best for: Capability Gaps, Cultural Resistance.
Lever 06
Realign
Focus on Results
▼

Adjusts metrics and rewards so they match current strategic priorities. People do what they are measured on — fix the incentives and the energy shifts.

Apply when: Effort is flowing to what is measured, not what matters. Best for: Misaligned Metrics & Incentives.
Lever 07
Rebuild Trust
Restore Credibility
▼

Heals relationships and introduces the transparency needed for people to commit again. Trust is the social grease of execution — without it, the system seizes up.

Apply when: Psychological safety or leadership credibility is the constraint. Best for: Cultural Resistance, Leadership Avoidance.

The 7 COMPASS High-Flammability Zones
Select each zone

By locating clogs within COMPASS, you move from reactive problem-solving to precise system intervention. Select each zone to explore where friction typically accumulates.

C
Clarity of Direction
▼

Signals confusion, drift, or competing priorities. When this zone heats up, people are unclear what strategy requires of them day-to-day.

Warning signals: Strategy described differently by each leader; too many unranked priorities; teams treat strategy as annual event; people wait for clarity before acting.
O
Organisational Alignment
▼

Signals fragmentation across functions. Different parts of the organisation execute to different priorities or conflicting incentives.

Warning signals: KPIs conflict across functions; incentives reward wrong behaviours; silos resist shared outcomes; customer issues bounce between teams.
M
Management Discipline
▼

Signals weak follow-through and accountability. Governance produces reports rather than decisions, and performance gaps are tolerated rather than corrected.

Warning signals: No clear review cadence; decision rights unclear; leaders tolerate repeated misses; accountability conversations avoided.
P
People & Capability
▼

Signals execution strain or capacity gaps. Strategy is demanding capabilities the organisation has not yet built or deployed.

Warning signals: Strategy requires skills not available; over-reliance on consultants without transfer; people fear speaking up; performance standards vary.
A
Allocation of Resources
▼

Signals misalignment between stated priorities and actual investment decisions. Money and people are flowing to the wrong places relative to strategy.

Warning signals: Budget follows history not priorities; too many projects funded simultaneously; leaders commit to outcomes without resourcing.
S1
Systems & Execution
▼

Signals operational or structural friction slowing execution throughput. The machinery of delivery is creating drag rather than enabling flow.

Warning signals: Operating model doesn’t support strategic priorities; critical work delayed by approvals; tools slow delivery; firefighting feels normal.
S2
Sensing & Adaptation
▼

Signals missed or ignored feedback. The organisation is operating on stale information and has lost its ability to adapt in real time.

Warning signals: Customer churn visible but ignored; competitor moves tracked late; leaders defend outdated forecasts; people hide bad news; teams stick to plans when reality has shifted.

Select the Type of Friction

Review the 7 clog categories and select the one that best describes the execution blockage. This is the most important step — an incorrectly classified clog will attract an incorrectly selected lever.

Ask: “Is this a structure problem, a capability problem, a decision problem, a metrics problem, a culture problem, a technology problem, or a leadership problem?”

Pinpoint Where in the System

Identify the COMPASS zone where the clog is forming. The combination of clog + zone provides diagnostic precision:

Select and Apply the Lever

Match the lever to the root cause — not to the symptom. The lever must address what is actually blocking flow.

  • Standardise — establish consistent frameworks
  • Streamline — simplify workflows
  • Clarify — define roles and decision rights
  • Upgrade — modernise systems or infrastructure
  • Reinforce — build capability through coaching
  • Realign — adjust incentives and metrics
  • Rebuild Trust — restore credibility through visible action
Prioritise the Intervention
  • Low — early signal; monitor and pre-empt
  • Medium — clear friction; act within 5 working days
  • High — significant risk; escalate to Alignment Brigade immediately

Severity rating allows the team to prioritise across the full strategy portfolio and build the Enterprise Unclogging Agenda.

Normalising the Unclog — From Crisis to Habit

The purpose of the Integration Zone is to standardise how the leadership team finds and fixes blockages. By using a shared unclogging process, the entire Alignment Brigade operates with a common language and a reliable toolkit. Fixing execution friction becomes a normal part of how the organisation runs.

When “unclogging” is a standard discipline, flagging a problem is no longer seen as a failure or a critique of a colleague. It is treated as valuable data. Detecting friction early is an act of leadership stewardship — not departmental interference.

The shared diagnostic path: Is there a clog? → What category? → Which COMPASS zone? → Which lever?

Silent Individual Preparation

Identify one KPI or Key Result from your current portfolio that is below target or stalled — and has not been resolved despite being visible for more than two review cycles.

Individual Preparation
Diagnostic Triads — 10 Minutes Per Person

Work in triads: Diagnostician · Challenger · Observer. Roles rotate so every participant runs the diagnostic once.

👤 Diagnostician

Presents the stalled KPI. Reasons aloud through the four questions. Must not skip Q3 — the clog must be named before the lever is selected.

👤 Challenger

Guides through the four questions in sequence. Challenges comfortable answers with: “Are you sure that’s the root — or is that the symptom of something deeper?”

👤 Observer

Evaluates diagnostic quality against the four criteria. Provides structured feedback after each round.

Q1 — Where is the friction?

Which COMPASS zone best describes where the pain is showing up?

Q2 — What is the signal?

Which signal in that zone most precisely describes what you are observing?

Q3 — What is the clog?

Based on that signal — structure, capability, decision, metrics, culture, technology, or leadership?

Q4 — What lever would you pull?

Specific action, with an owner and a date. Not a general direction.

The Alignment Brigade Challenge

Your triad nominates one diagnosis to bring to the full group. Present in 90 seconds:

“Our KR was [X]. The friction zone was [Y]. The signal was [Z]. We diagnosed [clog]. Our proposed lever is [action] — owned by [person] — by [date].”

The group will challenge: Is the zone right? Is this the root or a surface symptom? Is the lever concrete enough?

The facilitator will close with: “What would have to be true for that lever to actually clear the clog?”

Awareness

A Strategic Clog is an internal blockage that prevents strategic intent from flowing into execution. The 7-clog diagnostic map gives leaders the vocabulary to name friction precisely — aligned to ABCV Directional Integrity — rather than reacting to surface symptoms.

Intelligence

Clogs are design signals, not failures. Where strategy stretches the operating system beyond its current capacity, friction will appear predictably. The 7 levers restore the structural, behavioural, and operational conditions that allow strategy to flow at the intended speed.
