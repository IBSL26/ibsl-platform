Module 2 · Awareness — What Behavioural Strategy Is

Where traditional strategy asks 'What should we do?' — Behavioural Strategy asks the prior question: 'How does the way we think shape what we believe we should do — and are those thinking patterns helping or hurting us?'

The Dual-Process Framework

Daniel Kahneman's framework reveals that strategic thinking is a dynamic interaction between two internal systems. Understanding which system is dominant in your leadership team is the difference between a reactive impulse and a disciplined choice.

System 1 — Fast Thinking

  • Automatic and intuitive
  • Pattern recognition from experience
  • Produces rapid, confident judgements
  • Vulnerable to cognitive biases

System 2 — Slow Thinking

  • Deliberate and analytical
  • Weighs options and trade-offs
  • More reliable for complex problems
  • Requires psychological safety

Module 3 · Intelligence — Why It Is Critical

Research by McKinsey indicates organisations with disciplined decision-making practices deliver returns approximately 6% higher than peers. The hidden cost of poor behavioural awareness is not random — it follows predictable patterns across five strategic costs.

The Five Hidden Costs

Misallocated Capital

  • Sunk-Cost Fallacy
  • Endowment Effect
  • Overconfidence driving ego acquisitions

Strategic Inertia

  • Anchoring to old metrics
  • Status Quo Bias
  • Loss Aversion preventing new models

Execution Failure

  • Planning Fallacy in high-level plans
  • Optimism Bias in forecasting
  • Action-reinforcing Confirmation Bias

Innovation Suppression

  • Groupthink filtering transformative ideas
  • Career-Risk Bias silencing dissent
  • Conformity Bias to dominant view

Module 3 Reflection

Module 4 · Extrapolating — Where Behavioural Strategy Operates

Bias does not live in people alone. It lives in processes — in the specific moments within a strategy process where human judgement is required. Each of the four phases creates a distinct opening through which systematic distortion can enter.

Your CXO Perspective — The Four Scanner Questions

Apply each question to your current strategy cycle from your assigned role.

Diagnosis: If we stripped out everything our team already believed, what would the evidence actually tell us?

Option Generation: What is the most disruptive option we have not yet put on the table — and what is the real reason it is not there?

Decision: If the person who proposed this option had no stake in the outcome, would we make the same decision?

Execution: Are we measuring what matters to the strategy, or what is easy to measure?

Module 4 Reflection

In which of the four phases is your organisation most exposed to bias right now? What would you do differently if you were designing the process from scratch?
